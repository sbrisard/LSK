B.1 Proof of Eq. (4.5)

We first prove a preliminary identity. For fixed \(i = 1, \ldots, m\), Eq. (4.2) is of the form \(L_{jk} \, \xi_j \, \xi_k = 0\) for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Derivation with respect to \(\xi_l\) leads to \[ 0 = L_{jk} \, \delta_{jl} \, \xi_k + L_{jk} \, \xi_j \, \delta_{kl} = L_{lk} \, \xi_k + L_{jl} \, \xi_j = \bigl(L_{kl} +L_{lk} \bigr) \, \xi_l, \] for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals\). The above identity leads to: \(L_{kl} + L_{lk} = 0\). Application to Eq. (4.2) leads to \[ E_{ijk} + \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} + \lambda_j \, \dot{E}_{ik} = 0, \tag{B.1}\]

We now turn to Eq. (4.3), wich holds for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Plugging \(\xi_i = \order[1]{\xi_i}\) and using Eq. (4.1) delivers \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \bigl[ \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + 3\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{kl}) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + 2\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \bigl[ 2\dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) &= 0. \end{aligned} \tag{B.2}\]

Owing to the contraction with \(\order[1]{\xi}_j\), \(\order[1]{\xi}_k\) and \(\order[1]{\xi}_l\), the first term can be symmetrized with respect to \(j\), \(k\) and \(l\) \[ \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \bigl[ \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + 3\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{kl}) \bigr] = E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l, \] where \(E_{ijkl}\) is defined by Eq. (4.10). Similarly, the second term can be symmetrized with respect to \(j\) and \(k\) \[ \begin{aligned} 2 \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) &= \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \bigl[ \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \E_3(v_i, v_k, w_{j\lambda}) \bigr]\\ &= -\order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \bigl[ \E_2(w_{ij}, w_{k\lambda}) + \E_2(w_{ik}, w_{j\lambda}) \bigr]\\ &= \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_2(v_k, w_{ij}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_j, w_{ik}) \bigr], \end{aligned} \] where we have been testing Eq. (A.6) with \(\hat{w} = w_{k\lambda}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{j\lambda}\) and Eq. (A.7) with \(\hat{w} = w_{ij}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{ik}\), successively. Introducing \(\dot{E}_{ijk}\) defined by Eq. (4.9), the second term of Eq. (B.2) reduces to \[ \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + 2\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \bigr] = \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k. \]

The third term of Eq. (B.2) is simplified by testing Eq. (A.7), with \(\hat{w} = w_{j\lambda}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{i\lambda}\), successively \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + 2\dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) &= \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) -\E_2(w_{i\lambda}, w_{j\lambda})\\ &= \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \E_2(v_j, w_{i\lambda})\\ &= \ddot{E}_{ij}, \end{aligned} \] where \(\ddot{E}_{ij}\) is defined by Eq. (4.8). Gathering the above results delivers the following equation \[ \begin{aligned} \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Upon susbtitution of \(\order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl}\) with \(\order[2]{\lambda} - \lambda_k \, \order[2]{\xi_k}\) [see Eq. (4.1)] \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j - \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\xi}_k \, \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Eq. (B.1) is used to eliminate \(\lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij}\) \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\xi}_k \, \bigl( E_{ijk} + \lambda_j \, \dot{E}_{ik} \bigr) &= 0 \end{aligned} \] and Eq. (4.5) is retrieved.
