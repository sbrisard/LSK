7  Parametrization of the bifurcated branch

\[ \newcommand{\E}{\mathcal{E}} \newcommand{\order}[2][1]{#2^{(#1)}} \newcommand{\reals}{\mathbb{R}} \]

In this chapter, a parametrization of the bifurcated branch is introduced in order to prove Eqs. (3.1), (3.2), (3.3) and (3.4).

The bifurcated branch is a curve \((u, \lambda) \in \reals ^ {m + 1}\), which is parametrized by \(\eta\): \([u(\eta), \lambda(\eta)]\), with \(u(0) = u_0\) and \(\lambda(0) = \lambda_0\); primed quantities denoting derivatives with respect to \(\eta\), we introduce \[ \order[1]{\xi_i} = \xi_i'(0), \quad \order[2]{\xi_i} = \xi_i''(0), \quad \ldots, \quad \order[1]{\lambda} = \lambda'(0), \quad \ldots \]

In Section 6.3, \(\lambda\) was defined as a function of \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Therefore, from the chain rule \[ \lambda'(\eta) = \frac{\partial\lambda}{\partial\xi_i} \, \xi_i'(\eta) \quad \text{and} \quad \lambda''(\eta) = \frac{\partial^2\lambda}{\partial\xi_i \, \partial\xi_j} \, \xi_i'(\eta) \, \xi_j'(\eta) + \frac{\partial\lambda}{\partial\xi_i} \, \xi_i''(\eta) \] and, at \(\eta = 0\) \[ \order[1]{\lambda} = \lambda_i \, \order[1]{\xi_i} \quad \text{and} \quad \order[2]{\lambda} = \lambda_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi_i} \, \order[1]{\xi_j} + \lambda_i \, \order[2]{\xi_i}. \tag{7.1}\]

7.1 Simplification of the bifurcation equations

The bifurcation equations (6.8) and (6.9) have been derived in Chapter 6. Testing these equations with \(\hat{v} = v_i\) delivers \[ \xi_j \, \xi_k \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \, \E_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \lambda_k \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] = 0 \tag{7.2}\] and \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \xi_j \, \xi_k \, \xi_l \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{kl}) + \lambda_l \, \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \tfrac{1}{6} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) &\\ + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) + \lambda_k \, \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) &\\ + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_k \, \lambda_l \, \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] &= 0. \end{aligned} \tag{7.3}\] for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals\) and \(i = 1, \ldots, m\).

Eq. (7.2) holds for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Plugging \(\xi_i = \order[1]{\xi_i}\) and using Eq. (7.1) delivers the first bifurcation equation (3.3).

Similarly, Eq. (7.3) is simplified below to the second bifurcation equation (3.4). To do so, we first prove a preliminary identity. For fixed \(i = 1, \ldots, m\), Eq. (7.2) is of the form \(L_{jk} \, \xi_j \, \xi_k = 0\) for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Derivation with respect to \(\xi_l\) leads to \[ 0 = L_{jk} \, \delta_{jl} \, \xi_k + L_{jk} \, \xi_j \, \delta_{kl} = L_{lk} \, \xi_k + L_{jl} \, \xi_j = \bigl(L_{kl} +L_{lk} \bigr) \, \xi_l, \] for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals\). The above identity leads to: \(L_{kl} + L_{lk} = 0\). Application to Eq. (7.2) leads to \[ E_{ijk} + \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} + \lambda_j \, \dot{E}_{ik} = 0, \tag{7.4}\]

Going back to Eq. (7.3), wich holds for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Plugging \(\xi_i = \order[1]{\xi_i}\) and using Eq. (7.1) delivers \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \bigl[ \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + 3\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{kl}) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + 2\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \bigl[ 2\dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) &= 0. \end{aligned} \tag{7.5}\]

Owing to the contraction with \(\order[1]{\xi}_j\), \(\order[1]{\xi}_k\) and \(\order[1]{\xi}_l\), the first term can be symmetrized with respect to \(j\), \(k\) and \(l\) \[ \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \bigl[ \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + 3\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{kl}) \bigr] = E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l, \] where \(E_{ijkl}\) is defined by Eq. (2.10). Similarly, the second term can be symmetrized with respect to \(j\) and \(k\) \[ \begin{aligned} 2 \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) &= \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \bigl[ \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \E_3(v_i, v_k, w_{j\lambda}) \bigr]\\ &= -\order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \bigl[ \E_2(w_{ij}, w_{k\lambda}) + \E_2(w_{ik}, w_{j\lambda}) \bigr]\\ &= \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_2(v_k, w_{ij}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_j, w_{ik}) \bigr], \end{aligned} \] where we have been testing Eq. (2.4) with \(\hat{w} = w_{k\lambda}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{j\lambda}\) and Eq. (2.5) with \(\hat{w} = w_{ij}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{ik}\), successively. Introducing \(\dot{E}_{ijk}\) defined by Eq. (2.9), the second term of Eq. (7.5) reduces to \[ \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + 2\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \bigr] = \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k. \]

The third term of Eq. (7.5) is simplified by testing Eq. (2.5), with \(\hat{w} = w_{j\lambda}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{i\lambda}\), successively \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + 2\dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) &= \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) -\E_2(w_{i\lambda}, w_{j\lambda})\\ &= \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \E_2(v_j, w_{i\lambda})\\ &= \ddot{E}_{ij}, \end{aligned} \] where \(\ddot{E}_{ij}\) is defined by Eq. (2.7). Gathering the above results delivers the following equation \[ \begin{aligned} \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Upon susbtitution of \(\order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl}\) with \(\order[2]{\lambda} - \lambda_k \, \order[2]{\xi_k}\) [see Eq. (7.1)] \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j - \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\xi}_k \, \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Eq. (7.4) is used to eliminate \(\lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij}\) \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\xi}_k \, \bigl( E_{ijk} + \lambda_j \, \dot{E}_{ik} \bigr) &= 0 \end{aligned} \] and Eq. (3.4) is retrieved.

7.2 Taylor expansion of the bifurcated branch

We now turn to \(w\), which was defined in Section 6.2 as an implicit function of \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\) and \(\lambda\). It is now defined as a function of the arc parameter \(\eta\) as follows: \(w(n) = w[\xi_1(\eta), \ldots, \xi_m(\eta), \lambda(\eta)]\). From the chain rule \[ w'(\eta) = \frac{\partial w}{\partial \xi_i} \, \xi_i' + \frac{\partial w}{\partial \lambda} \, \lambda' \] and \[ w''(\eta) = \frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial \xi_i \, \partial \xi_j} \, \xi_i' \, \xi_j' + 2\frac{\partial w}{\partial \xi_i \, \partial \lambda} \, \xi_i' \, \lambda' + \frac{\partial w}{\partial \xi_i} \, \xi_i'' + \frac{\partial^2 w}{\partial \lambda^2} \, \lambda^{'2} + \frac{\partial w}{\partial \lambda} \, \lambda''. \]

At \(\eta = 0\), the above identities reduce to \[ w'(0) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad w''(0) = \order[1]{\xi_i} \, \order[1]{\xi_j} \, w_{ij} + 2 \order[1]{\lambda} \, \order[1]{\xi_i} \, w_{i\lambda} \]

We deduce from the above the Taylor expansion (3.1) of the bifurcated branch as \(\eta \to 0\).
