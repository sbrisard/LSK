4  Parametrization of the bifurcated branch

\[ \newcommand{\E}{\mathcal{E}} \newcommand{\order}[2][1]{#2^{(#1)}} \newcommand{\reals}{\mathbb{R}} \]

Testing the bifurcation equations (A.10) and (A.11) with \(\hat{v} = v_i\) delivers \[ \xi_j \, \xi_k \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \, \E_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \lambda_k \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] = 0 \tag{4.1}\] and \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \xi_j \, \xi_k \, \xi_l \, \bigl[ \lambda_l \, \E_3(v_i, w_{j\lambda}, v_k) + \tfrac{1}{6} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + \lambda_k \, \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) &\\ + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_k \, \lambda_l \, \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j)\bigr] &= 0, \end{aligned} \tag{4.2}\] for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals\). For fixed \(i = 1, \ldots, m\), Eq. 4.1 is of the form \(L_{jk} \, \xi_j \, \xi_k = 0\) for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Derivation with respect to \(\xi_l\) leads to \[ 0 = L_{jk} \, \delta_{jl} \, \xi_k + L_{jk} \, \xi_j \, \delta_{kl} = L_{lk} \, \xi_k + L_{jl} \, \xi_j = \bigl(L_{kl} +L_{lk} \bigr) \, \xi_l, \] for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals\). The above identity leads to: \(L_{kl} + L_{lk} = 0\). Application to Eq. 4.1 leads to \[ E_{ijk} + \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} + \lambda_j \, \dot{E}_{ik} = 0, \tag{4.3}\] with \[ \dot{E}_{ij} = \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \quad \text{and} \quad E_{ijk} = \E_3(v_i, v_j, v_k). \]

To transform the above equations, a parametrization of the bifurcated branch is introduced. The bifurcated branch is a curve \((u, \lambda) \in \reals ^ {m + 1}\), which is parametrized by \(\eta\): \([u(\eta), \lambda(\eta)]\), with \(u(0) = u_0\) and \(\lambda(0) = \lambda_0\); primed quantities denoting derivatives with respect to \(\eta\), we introduce \[ \order[1]{\xi_i} = \xi_i'(0), \quad \order[2]{\xi_i} = \xi_i''(0), \quad \ldots, \quad \order[1]{\lambda} = \lambda'(0), \quad \ldots \]

In Section A.4.2, \(\lambda\) was defined as a function of \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Therefore, from the chain rule \[ \lambda'(\eta) = \frac{\partial\lambda}{\partial\xi_i} \, \xi_i'(\eta) \quad \text{and} \quad \lambda''(\eta) = \frac{\partial^2\lambda}{\partial\xi_i \, \partial\xi_j} \, \xi_i'(\eta) \, \xi_j'(\eta) + \frac{\partial\lambda}{\partial\xi_i} \, \xi_i''(\eta) \] and, at \(\eta = 0\) \[ \order[1]{\lambda} = \lambda_i \, \order[1]{\xi_i} \quad \text{and} \quad \order[2]{\lambda} = \lambda_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi_i} \, \order[1]{\xi_j} + \lambda_i \, \order[2]{\xi_i}. \tag{4.4}\]

Eq. (4.1) holds for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Plugging \(\xi_i = \order[1]{\xi_i}\) and using Eq. (4.4) delivers \[ E_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi_j} \, \order[1]{\xi_k} + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi_j} = 0 \]

Eq. (4.2) is transformed similarly \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \E_3(v_i, w_{j\lambda}, v_k) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr] ^2 \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \, \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) \bigr] &\\ + \tfrac{1}{6} \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + \tfrac{1}{2} \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) &= 0, \end{aligned} \] substituting \(\order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl}\) with \(\order[2]{\lambda} - \lambda_k \, \order[2]{\xi_k}\) [see Eq. (4.4)] \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \E_3(v_i, w_{j\lambda}, v_k) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr] ^2 \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \, \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) \bigr] &\\ + \tfrac{1}{6} \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + \tfrac{1}{2} \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \bigl( \order[2]{\lambda} - \lambda_k \, \order[2]{\xi_k} \bigr) \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) &= 0 \end{aligned} \] and rearranging \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + 2\E_3(v_i, w_{j\lambda}, v_k) \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k &\\ + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \bigl[ \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + 2\dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j - \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \order[2]{\xi_k} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Testing Eq. (A.7), with \(\hat{w} = w_{j\lambda}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{i\lambda}\), successively, we get \[ \dot{\E}_2(w_{j\lambda}, v_i) = -\E_2(w_{i\lambda}, w_{j\lambda}) = -\E_2(w_{j\lambda}, w_{i\lambda}) = \dot{\E}_2(w_{i\lambda}, v_j) \] and \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + 2\E_3(v_i, w_{j\lambda}, v_k) \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k &\\ + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j - \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \order[2]{\xi_k} &= 0. \end{aligned} \] with \[ \ddot{E}_{ij} = \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \dot{\E}_2(w_{i\lambda}, v_j). \]

Owing to contraction with \(\order[1]{\xi}_j\) and \(\order[1]{\xi}_k\), the second term can be symmetrized with respect to \(j\) and \(k\). More precisely, testing Eq. (A.7) with \(\hat{w} = w_{j\lambda}\) and \(\hat{w} = w_{k\lambda}\), successively, \[ \begin{aligned}[b] 2\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_k) \, \order[1]{\xi_j} \order[1]{\xi_k} &= \bigl[ \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_k) + \E_3(v_i, w_j, v_k) \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi_j} \order[1]{\xi_k}\\ &= \bigl[ \dot{\E}_2(v_k, w_{ij}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_j, w_{ik}) \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi_j} \order[1]{\xi_k}. \end{aligned} \]

\[ \dot{E}_{ijk} = \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_j, w_{ik}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_k, w_{ij}). \]

\[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l - \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k - \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \order[2]{\xi_k} &\\ + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Eq. 4.3 is used to eliminate \(\lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij}\) \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l - \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl( E_{ijk} + \lambda_j \, \dot{E}_{ik} \bigr) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\xi_k} &\\ + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0, \end{aligned} \] and, using Eq. 4.4 \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l - \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k &\\ + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \bigr] \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \bigl( E_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \bigr) \, \order[2]{\xi}_j + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j&= 0, \end{aligned} \]

4.1 Bookmark

We now turn to \(w\) \[\begin{equation} w'(\eta) = w_{,i} \, \xi_i' + w_{,\lambda} \, \lambda' \quad \text{and} \quad w''(\eta) = w_{,ij} \, \xi_i' \, \xi_j' + 2w_{,i\lambda} \, \xi_i' \, \lambda' + w_{,i} \, \xi_i'' + w_{,\lambda\lambda} \, \lambda^{'2} + w_{,\lambda} \, \lambda'' \end{equation}\] and, at \(\eta = 0\) \[\begin{equation} w'(0) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad w''(0) = \order[1]{\xi_i} \, \order[1]{\xi_j} \, w_{ij} + 2 \order[1]{\lambda} \, \order[1]{\xi_i} \, w_i \end{equation}\] and we get the Taylor expansion of the bifurcated branch as \(\eta \to 0\) \[\begin{equation} u(\eta) = u^\ast[\lambda(\eta)] + \order[1]{\xi_i} \, v_i + \tfrac{1}{2} \bigl( \order[2]{\xi_i} \, v_i + \order[1]{\xi_i} \, \order[1]{\xi_j} \, w_{ij} + 2\order[1]{\lambda} \, \order[1]{\xi_i} \, w_i\bigr) + o(\eta^2), \end{equation}\] to be compared with Eq.~\(\eqref{eq:20220524134613}\).
