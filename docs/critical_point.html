Analysis of the critical point

2. Analysis of the critical point#

In this section, we discuss the stability of the critical point \((u_0, \lambda_0)\). To this end, we evaluate the potential energy in a neighboring state \(u_0 + u\), where \(u \in U\) is ``small’’. We have, to the fourth order

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned}[b] \E(u_0 + u, \lambda_0) - \E(u_0, \lambda_0) ={} &\tfrac{1}{2} \E_2(\lambda_0; u, u) + \tfrac{1}{6} \E_3(\lambda_0; u, u, u)\\ &+ \tfrac{1}{24} \E_4(\lambda_0; u, u, u, u) + o(\langle u , u \rangle^2), \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

where the linear term has been omitted, \(u_0\) being a critical point of the energy. Since \(v \in V\), we have \(\E_2(\lambda_0; v, \bullet) = 0\). We now expand \(u\) as \(u = \xi \, v + \eta \, w\), with \(\xi, \eta \in \reals\) and \(v \in V\) and \(w \in W\) are fixed, orthogonal directions. Owing to the multi-linearity and symmetry of the successive differential of \(\E\), the above expression expands as follows

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned}[b] \E(u_0 + u, \lambda_0) - \E(u_0, \lambda_0) ={} & \tfrac{1}{2} \eta^2 \, \E_2(\lambda_0; w, w) + \tfrac{1}{6} \xi^3 \, \E_3(\lambda_0; v, v, v)\\ & + \tfrac{1}{2} \xi^2 \eta \E_3(\lambda_0; v, v, w) + \tfrac{1}{2} \xi \eta^2 \E_3(\lambda_0; v, w, w)\\ & + \tfrac{1}{6} \eta^3 \E_3(\lambda_0; w, w, w) + \tfrac{1}{24} \xi^4 \E_4(\lambda_0; v, v, v, v)\\ & + \tfrac{1}{6} \xi^3 \eta \E_4(\lambda_0; v, v, v, w) + \tfrac{1}{4} \xi^2 \eta^2 \E_4(\lambda_0; v, v, w, w)\\ & + \tfrac{1}{6} \xi \eta^3 \E_4(\lambda_0; v, w, w, w) + \tfrac{1}{24} \eta^4 \E_4(\lambda_0; w, w, w, w)\\ & + o\bigl[\bigl(\xi^2 + \eta^2\bigr)^2\bigr]. \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

For the equilibrium to be stable, the above expression must be \(\geq 0\) for all \(\xi\) et \(\eta\) small enough. Taking first \(\eta = 0\), we get the following necessary conditions

(2.1)#\[ \E_3(\lambda_0; v, v, v) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \E_4(\lambda_0; v, v, v, v) \geq 0 \quad \text{for all} \quad v \in V.\]

Note

Note that, from Theorem \ref{thr:20220802112835}, the first of these two conditions is equivalent to \(E_{ijk}=0\), for all \(i, j, k = 1, \ldots m\).

In other words, if there exists \(v \in V\) such that \(\E_3(\lambda_0; v, v, v) \neq 0\) or \(\E_4(v, v, v, v) < 0\), then the equilibrium is \emph{unstable} at the critical point. The above conditions are not sufficient. Indeed, assuming conditions (2.1) to hold, we now take \(\eta = \xi^2\)

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned}[b] \E(u_0 + u, \lambda_0) - \E(u_0, \lambda_0) ={} & \tfrac{1}{2} \xi^4 \bigl[ \E_2(\lambda_0; w, w) + \E_3(\lambda_0; v, v, w)\\ & + \tfrac{1}{12} \E_4(\lambda_0; v, v, v, v) \bigr] + o(\xi^4) \end{aligned}\end{split}\]

and we get the further necessary condition

(2.2)#\[ \E_2(w, w) + \E_3(v, v, w) + \tfrac{1}{12} \E_4(v, v, v, v) \geq 0 \quad \text{for all} \quad v \in V \quad \text{and} \quad w \in W.\]

The direction \(v \in V\) being fixed, the above expression is minimal when \(w\) satisfies the following variational problem

(2.3)#\[ 2\E_2(w, \hat{w}) +\E_3(v, v, \hat{w}) = 0 \quad \text{for all} \quad \hat{w} \in W.\]

Expanding \(v \in V\) in the \((v_i)\) basis as follows: \(v = \xi_i v_i\), it is observed that the solution to the above variational problem is \(w = \tfrac{1}{2} \xi_i \xi_j w_{ij}\), where \(w_{ij}\) is the solution to the elementary variational problem (1.6). For this value of \(w\), condition (2.2) reads

\[ \bigl[\E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) - 3\E_2(w_{ij}, w_{kl})\bigr] \xi_i \xi_j \xi_k \xi_l \geq 0 \quad \text{for all} \quad \xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals,\]

which, in view of definition (1.12) of \(E_{ijkl}\), is equivalent to

\[ E_{ijkl} \xi_i \xi_j \xi_k \xi_l \geq 0 \quad \text{for all} \quad \xi_m, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals.\]

Note that Eq. (2.3) implies \(\E_4(\lambda_0; v, v, v, v) \geq 0\), which becomes a redundant necessary condition. Indeed, plugging \(w= \xi_i \xi_j w_{ij}\) into Eq. (2.3) cancels the first two terms. To sum up, we have the following \emph{necessary} conditions for stability

\[ E_{ijk} \xi_i \xi_j \xi_k = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad E_{ijkl} \xi_i \xi_j \xi_k \xi_l \geq 0 \quad \text{for all} \quad \xi_m, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals.\]

\pdfmarkupcomment{Conversely, the following condition is \emph{sufficient} to ensure stability of the critical point}{`A d’emontrer}

\[ E_{ijk} \xi_i \xi_j \xi_k = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad E_{ijkl} \xi_i \xi_j \xi_k \xi_l > 0 \quad \text{for all} \quad \xi_m, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals.\]
