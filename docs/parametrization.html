6.1 Simplification of the bifurcation equations

The bifurcation equations (5.8) and (5.9) have been derived in Chapter 5. Testing these equations with \(\hat{v} = v_i\) delivers \[ \xi_j \, \xi_k \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \, \E_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \lambda_k \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] = 0 \tag{6.1}\] and \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \xi_j \, \xi_k \, \xi_l \, \bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2} \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{kl}) + \lambda_l \, \E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \tfrac{1}{6} \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) &\\ + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) + \lambda_k \, \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) &\\ + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_l \, \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + \tfrac{1}{2} \lambda_k \, \lambda_l \, \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] &= 0. \end{aligned} \tag{6.2}\] for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m \in \reals\) and \(i = 1, \ldots, m\).

Similarly, Eq. (6.2) is simplified below to the second bifurcation equation (3.4). To do so, we first prove a preliminary identity.

Going back to Eq. (6.2), wich holds for all \(\xi_1, \ldots, \xi_m\). Plugging \(\xi_i = \order[1]{\xi_i}\) and using Eq. (5.10) delivers \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \bigl[ \E_4(v_i, v_j, v_k, v_l) + 3\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{kl}) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\lambda} \, \bigl[ \dot{\E}_3(v_i, v_j, v_k) + 2\E_3(v_i, v_j, w_{k\lambda}) + \dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{jk}) \bigr] &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \bigl[ 2\dot{\E}_2(v_i, w_{j\lambda}) + \ddot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) \bigr] + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{\E}_2(v_i, v_j) &= 0. \end{aligned} \tag{6.3}\]

Gathering the above results delivers the following equation \[ \begin{aligned} \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl} \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Upon susbtitution of \(\order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l \, \lambda_{kl}\) with \(\order[2]{\lambda} - \lambda_k \, \order[2]{\xi_k}\) [see Eq. (5.10)] \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j - \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\xi}_k \, \lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij} &= 0. \end{aligned} \]

Eq. (5.11) is used to eliminate \(\lambda_k \, \dot{E}_{ij}\) \[ \begin{aligned}[b] \tfrac{1}{3} E_{ijkl} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k \, \order[1]{\xi}_l + \order[1]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ijk} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[1]{\xi}_k + \bigl[ \order[1]{\lambda} \bigr]^2 \, \ddot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j &\\ + \order[2]{\lambda} \, \dot{E}_{ij} \, \order[1]{\xi}_j + \order[1]{\xi}_j \, \order[2]{\xi}_k \, \bigl( E_{ijk} + \lambda_j \, \dot{E}_{ik} \bigr) &= 0 \end{aligned} \] and Eq. (3.4) is retrieved.
